EONA Charter

This charter declares the fundamental rights of a person who exists outside their birth identity.

A human is a human even when a passport is gone.

⸻

Articles

Article 1 – Right to exist
A person has the right to exist without permission from a state.

Article 2 – Right to identity
Identity is a human right, not a government privilege.

Article 3 – Right to memory
A human has the right to preserve their history, culture and relationships regardless of borders.

Article 4 – Right to safety
A person escaping persecution must not be punished for their existence.

Article 5 – Right to freedom of belief
A human can choose faith even when faith is illegal.

Article 6 – Right to recognition
Community recognition is valid identity.

Article 7 – Right to digital continuity
Digital identity must survive displacement.

Article 8 – Right to belonging
Humans deserve a community even if nations reject them.

Article 9 – Right to choose nationality
Nationality is a choice, not a birth accident.

Article 10 – Right to identity inheritance
A person’s identity can be passed on, protected and verified across generations.
